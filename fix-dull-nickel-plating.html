Fix Dull Nickel Plating

Common causes of flat, gray, or lifeless nickel deposits and how to correct them.

Check cleaning first

Most dull finishes come from oil, oxide, or residue left on the surface. Re-clean the part and avoid touching it before plating.

Increase current carefully

A nickel deposit can stay dull if current density is too low. Increase in small steps while watching for a smoother, brighter appearance.

Look at agitation and spacing

Weak bath movement and poor anode placement can starve the surface and reduce brightness. Add gentle agitation and improve part orientation.

Evaluate bath age

If the bath has seen repeated use, brightener activity may be low. A brightener additive can restore cosmetic performance when prep and current are already correct.
