Electroplating Guide: Please read the entire guide before starting.

⚠ Safety First:

Wear gloves, safety goggles, and protective clothing.

Work in a well-ventilated area.

Avoid skin contact and keep away from children.

1. Prepare the Plating Bath

✅ Use a plastic or glass container (NO metal).

✅ Add nickel plating solution to the container.

✅ Heat the solution to 125-140°F (52-60°C) for best results.

✅ Agitate the solution continuously by using an aquarium pump for gentle circulation or by

     stirring gently every few minutes with a plastic or glass rod.

2. Setup the Electroplating System

✅ Connect the nickel anode (+) to the positive (red) wire of your power supply.

✅ Connect the item to be plated (-) to the negative (black) wire. (after cleaned)

✅ Place the anode and item in the solution, ensuring they don’t touch. (don’t allow alligator clips to touch the solution. ). For optimal results, use multiple anodes spaced evenly.  

3. Cleaning the Part (Very Important!)

✅ Wire Brushing – Use a brass or stainless steel wire brush to remove flaky or loose metal.

✅ Sandpaper or Scotch-Brite Pads – Lightly sand the surface to smooth out any rough spots. For best results get down to bare metal.

✅ Clean the item thoroughly with degreaser, caustic soda  or dish soap then rinse.

✅(Optional but for best results)  acid dip (hydrochloric acid or muriatic acid)  for 15--60 seconds, then rinse thoroughly.

✅ Once cleaning process has started, only handle with latex/nitrile/rubber gloves to avoid hand oil contamination.

4. Electroplating Process

✅ Set the power supply to 0.07-0.1 amps per square inch of surface area.

✅ Slowly increase power until you see a smooth, even plating.

✅ Plate for 15-60 minutes, depending on thickness desired.

✅ Stir or gently agitate the solution occasionally for even coverage.

5. Finishing & Polishing

✅ Remove the part and rinse with distilled water.

✅ Buff with a soft cloth or polish for a bright finish.

✅ Optional: Seal with a clear lacquer for extra durability.

Troubleshooting Tips

1.Uneven Plating (Dark or Light Patches)

  • Poor agitation or solution circulation
  • Uneven current distribution
  • Improper cleaning of the substrate
  • Contaminants in the solution
  • Fixes: Circulate solution, put multiple anodes around part to be plated (spaced evenly), filter solution through Polypropylene filter bags / 5-10 microns. (This solution is free of impurities when first used)

2. Poor Adhesion (Peeling or Flaking)

  • Insufficient cleaning/pre-treatment | clean only after bare metal is showing.
  • Improper surface activation | Dip in hydrochloric acid for 15-60 seconds
  • Low chloride levels affecting anode efficiency | Add nickel chloride to replenish
  • Too high or low pH |  Adjust pH
  • Fixes: Properly degrease and acid-clean.

3. Rough or Grainy Finish

  • Causes: High current density, contaminants, improper boric acid levels.
  • Fixes: Lower amperage, filter solution (1-5 micron filter), add 4-6 oz/gal boric acid.
